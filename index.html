About This Project

Research Overview

AlgoSched addresses the complexity of academic timetabling — an NP-hard combinatorial problem — using a hybrid approach combining Elitist Genetic Algorithm (EGA) for global search and Constraint Satisfaction Problem (CSP) techniques for feasibility enforcement.

Developed specifically for the College of Computer Studies (CCS) of Rizal College of Taal, the system automates scheduling for multiple year levels, sections, and subjects while respecting institutional constraints.

The Elitist GA preserves the best solutions (elites) each generation, preventing fitness regression — a critical improvement over standard GAs for scheduling problems.

EGA Process Flow

  • 01Initialize random population of schedule chromosomes
  • 02Evaluate fitness using constraint satisfaction score
  • 03Select elite chromosomes (top 15%) for preservation
  • 04Tournament selection for parent chromosomes
  • 05Single-point crossover to produce offspring
  • 06Random mutation to maintain diversity
  • 07Replace population, keep elites intact
  • 08Repeat until convergence or 500 generations
